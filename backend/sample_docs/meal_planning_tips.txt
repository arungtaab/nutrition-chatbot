Meal planning — vegetarian kitchen first, mixed tables welcome

I built this headspace around **vegetarian** cooking at home—**no meat, poultry, fish, or eggs** for me—while getting honest about **Sunday ambition vs Wednesday meltdown**. The habits below work if you’re omnivore too; you just rotate in the animal proteins your family eats.

Around your real week
- One planning block (Sunday, Wednesday night—whenever) for meals you’ll **actually** make.
- Batch the boring bits: grains, roasted vegetables, one sauce.
- Prep veg once so stir-fries and salads don’t feel like a project.

Plates — vegetarian baseline
- About half plants, quarter plant protein (beans, tofu, tempeh, dairy if you use it), quarter grains or starchy veg—flex when you’re hungrier or lighter.
- Rotate proteins so lentil fatigue doesn’t set in.

Plates — mixed or omnivore household
- Same visual balance; swap in grilled chicken, fish, or eggs on part of the batch while keeping a **veg-forward** side everyone can share.
- Cook one base (grain + veg) and split toppings so you’re not running two full menus.

Staples to keep around
- Pantry: beans, oats, rice/quinoa/pasta, olive oil, nuts, spices that still smell like something.
- Freezer: vegetables, bread, backup meals for dignity nights.
- Fridge: greens, tofu or tempeh, dairy if applicable, whatever protein your household uses.

Restrictions (not medical advice)
- **My lane:** legumes, soy, dairy, nuts/seeds cover protein without meat/fish/poultry/eggs.
- **Vegan:** drop dairy; mind B12/iron with a professional’s help.
- **More protein (any diet):** extra legumes, soy, dairy, eggs, fish, or meat as appropriate.
- **Low sodium:** low-salt cans, herbs, citrus; rinse canned beans.

One-sentence debriefs help: “Fiber from veg, protein from chickpeas, fat from tahini.” Sounds fussy until next week when you forget what worked.

Review weekly—swap one grain, one protein, one sauce. Good enough every week beats perfect never.
