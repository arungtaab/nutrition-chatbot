Meal planning tips for balanced nutrition

Meal planning saves time, reduces food waste, and makes it easier to eat a balanced diet. These tips help you build nutrition-aware meals and stay on track with your goals.

Plan around your schedule
- Choose a day each week to plan and prep (e.g. Sunday). Write down breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks.
- Batch-cook grains and proteins: cook a large pot of quinoa or rice, grill or bake chicken or tofu, and store in portions for the week.
- Prep vegetables in advance: wash, chop, and store in containers for quick stir-fries, salads, or roasting.

Balance each plate
- Aim for half vegetables or fruit, one-quarter lean protein, one-quarter whole grains or starchy vegetables.
- Vary proteins: rotate poultry, fish, legumes, and eggs to get a range of nutrients and avoid boredom.
- Include a source of healthy fat at each meal (nuts, seeds, avocado, olive oil) for satiety and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.

Keep staples on hand
- Pantry: canned beans, whole grains (oats, rice, quinoa), olive oil, nuts, seeds, herbs and spices.
- Freezer: frozen vegetables, fruit, and lean proteins for busy days.
- Fridge: fresh vegetables, eggs, yogurt, and a lean protein for the next few days.

Address preferences and restrictions
- Vegetarian/vegan: rely on legumes, tofu, tempeh, eggs (if applicable), nuts, and seeds for protein.
- More protein: add an extra portion of chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, or legumes; include nuts and seeds as snacks.
- Low sodium: use fresh or no-salt-added canned ingredients, herbs and spices instead of salt, and rinse canned beans.

Make it explainable
- When choosing a meal, think about why it works: “This lunch has fibre from quinoa and vegetables, protein from chickpeas, and healthy fat from olive oil.”
- Explain recommendations to others (or yourself) in terms of nutrients and balance, so choices are intentional and repeatable.

Review and adjust weekly. If a plan feels too heavy or light, adjust portions and swap in different grains, proteins, or vegetables. A sustainable plan is one you can stick to while meeting your nutrition goals.
