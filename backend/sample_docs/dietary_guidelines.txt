Dietary guidelines and meal structure

A balanced diet supports overall health and helps maintain a healthy weight. Meals are typically structured around three main eating occasions: breakfast, lunch, and dinner, with optional healthy snacks in between.

Key principles:
- Fill half your plate with vegetables and fruits. Aim for variety and colour.
- Choose whole grains (e.g. brown rice, oats, whole-wheat bread) for at least half of your grain intake.
- Include a source of lean protein at each meal: poultry, fish, legumes, eggs, or tofu.
- Add healthy fats in moderation: nuts, seeds, avocado, olive oil.
- Limit added sugars, sodium, and highly processed foods.

Portion control and regular meal timing can help with energy levels and appetite. Eating at roughly the same times each day supports digestion and can make it easier to plan nutritious meals.

For most adults, a typical day might include:
- Breakfast: whole grains, protein, and fruit (e.g. oatmeal with nuts and berries).
- Lunch: vegetables, lean protein, and a whole-grain side (e.g. salad with grilled chicken and quinoa).
- Dinner: similar balance with vegetables, protein, and a starch or grain, keeping portions moderate.

Adapt these guidelines to your calorie needs, activity level, and any medical or dietary restrictions. A healthcare provider or dietitian can help tailor a plan.
