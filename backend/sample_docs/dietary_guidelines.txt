Dietary guidelines — why this bundle exists, and who else can use it

**Why I wrote this:** I eat **vegetarian** with **no meat, poultry, fish, or eggs**—dairy’s fine for me, but adjust if you’re vegan. I wanted guidelines I could point a RAG corpus at without pretending one plate fits every culture or budget.

**If you eat differently:** same skeleton still helps—veg-heavy plates, whole grains, purposeful protein—just swap the protein lines for chicken, fish, eggs, or whatever matches your ethics and health plan.

Patterns that hold up for most people:
- Roughly **half** vegetables and fruit (fresh, frozen, canned all count).
- **Whole grains** when practical: oats, brown rice, breads that are actually whole grain.
- **Protein every meal** — for my lane: legumes, tofu, tempeh, dairy if you use it, nuts/seeds in sensible amounts. For omnivores: add lean meats, fish, eggs as fits.
- **Fats on purpose:** olive oil, avocado, tahini, nuts—so meals satisfy and carry flavor.
- **Sugar / salt / ultra-processed:** moderation, not a morality play.

Regular-ish meal times beat skipping lunch and panic-snacking at 4 p.m.—speaking from experience.

Rough day sketch (tweak portions and foods to you):
- Breakfast: grains + protein + fruit (e.g. oatmeal with nut butter and berries; or eggs and toast if that’s your household).
- Lunch: vegetables + protein + grain (bowl, sandwich with salad, leftovers).
- Dinner: same idea—balance, not performance.

Allergies, pregnancy, chronic conditions, meds: a clinician or registered dietitian beats any text file I dropped in a repo.
