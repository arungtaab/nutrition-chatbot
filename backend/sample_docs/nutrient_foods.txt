Nutrient cheat sheet — pantry staples (vegetarian origin, mixed-eater friendly)

This list started because I plan **vegetarian** plates with **no meat, fish, poultry, or eggs** and still ask: **where’s the protein? fiber? something besides white carbs?** I added a short **animal-protein** column so roommates, partners, or clone-and-share users can query omnivore meals and still hit the same RAG bundle.

Grains and starches
- Oats: fiber, B vitamins, magnesium; satiety.
- Brown rice: fiber, manganese.
- Quinoa: complete plant protein, fiber, iron.
- Whole-wheat bread/pasta: read labels—true whole grain, not marketing flour.

Plant proteins (my default)
- Lentils, chickpeas, beans: protein, fiber, iron/folate; cheap and batchable.
- Tofu and tempeh: protein; calcium if calcium-set.
- Greek yogurt, cottage cheese (if dairy works for you): protein, calcium.
- Nuts and seeds: protein and fat—portion-aware.

Animal proteins (if you or your household eats them)
- Chicken breast: lean protein, B vitamins.
- Fish (e.g. salmon): protein, omega-3s, vitamin D.
- Eggs: protein, choline, B12, vitamin D.

Vegetables and fruit
- Leafy greens: vitamins A/C/K, folate, iron—pair with vitamin C for iron absorption.
- Broccoli and brassicas: fiber, vitamin C.
- Sweet potato: fiber, beta-carotene, potassium.
- Berries: fiber, antioxidants; relatively lower sugar.
- Bananas: potassium; quick energy.

Pantry workhorses
- Canned beans (rinse if watching sodium).
- Olive oil; canned tomatoes/paste; tahini, miso, spices.

Use this for **vegetarian** weeks, **higher-fiber** goals, **mixed** households, or “what nutrient am I even looking at?” reference.
